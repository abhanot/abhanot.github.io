Jedi Mind Tricks

The US Department of Defense has released the final proposal for its $10 billion project, JEDI (Joint Enterprise Defense Infrastructure), an innovative cloud project that will revolutionize how the US fights wars. The project seeks to develop specialized cloud software for war machines and war-fighters (robotic soldiers), allowing them to better execute their missions by improving their data access and capabilities.

The budget for this project is incredible in size. Several tech giants are currently bidding for the $10 billion payout. Amazon currently has an advantage as they have the highest market share in cloud computing, with Amazon Web Services. Amazon also signed a contract with the US government in 2013 to create GovCloud, a massive isolated service capable of securely moving data between US government agencies. Other bidders such as Microsoft, Oracle, CSRA (General Dynamics), IBM, and Google, are Amazon’s major competitors. Microsoft could potentially be the perfect fit for this role due to its recent acquisitions in AI.

The JEDI project’s 10-year timeline is segmented into four contracts; a two-year, two three-year and a final two-year. Each of the four contracts may potentially be claimed by different bidders. The involvement of multiple cloud vendors in the JEDI project serves to distribute talent and preserve flexibility, minimizing the impacts of potential limitations experienced by any one company.

Algorithmic warfare introduces a new paradigm in combat. With JEDI, the US can use machine learning algorithms—predictive models reliant on vast sets of data—to radically transform the warfare industry. Currently, the Pentagon’s AI spending has been focused on autonomous weapons such as robotic soldiers that can kill without human input. This relates to Google’s controversial defense program, Project Maven, in which UAVs (Unmanned Aerial Vehicles) and drones were programmed to pinpoint potential threats, and would attack without direct command.

JEDI would permit all of the military’s data, along with machine learning algorithms, to be delivered to robotic soldiers through a cloud-based system. This would increase the warfighters’ data access and allow them to rapidly digest, analyze, and manipulate the information with much more robust machine learning models than can be stored on a single unit. The ability for warfighters to be programmed and updated via the cloud would keep them from falling behind as technology advances, without a lengthy manufacturing process. The JEDI project has the potential to revolutionize modern warfare.

One of the tech giants will sign and be approved to begin research. The project’s four terms will spark quarrels in Silicon Valley over the next decade. This is a significant opportunity for innovation in warfare, and JEDI will certainly be playing tricks along the way.
