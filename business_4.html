Financial Siri

How big banks are using A.I. to aid consumers and innovate business

Technology is paramount in our society, ranging from the advent of self-driving cars to advancements in cancer research. In North America, the use of AI has become commonplace with the emergence of technology that’s made our day-to-day lives easier, including Siri and Alexa, which were developed by Apple and Amazon respectively. AI is being implemented in the financial sector; specifically in banking. Two major banks, the Royal Bank of Canada (RBC) and Bank of America (BoA), have undertaken unique Research and Development efforts to change the operations and mechanics of modern banking.



Leading The Pack


BoA is the second-largest bank in the United States with total assets of $2.28 trillion, and earned revenues of $87 billion in 2017. BoA has created an online banking platform that makes daily transactions operate seamlessly through the use of AI. Major developments of online banking include the ability to pay bills from mobile devices, create payee(s), and send e-transfers (online transactions where money is transferred peer-to-peer with customized security questions). In order to improve and streamline its customer experience, BoA budgeted $3 billion to develop a digital assistant called Erica. Erica’s initial pilot launch took place in Rhode Island in March 2018, and the rest of the United States followed soon after. The digital assistant service proved to be a success among consumers, securing a million active users within the first three months of its launch. There is still massive room for growth; BoA has 25 million online banking users, and Erica has many capabilities, including searching transactions, budgeting, and determining credit scores. Michelle Moore, Head of Digital Banking at BoA, stated that a principle use of this AI in banking is searching transactions. Specifically, the technology can view prior transactions with retailers (i.e., Amazon, Ebay, Uber) as well as what form of payment was used. Erica’s other functions include monthly budgeting and the ability to choose to lock or unlock debit cards. BoA seems to have succeeded in integrating AI with modern banking.

What about Canada?

The second bank to consider is Canada’s largest public bank, RBC. It has the largest market capitalization in Canada and earned $40.7 billion in revenues in 2017. RBC continues to lead the movement towards a future of banking with AI as its centerpiece. Current Chief Executive Officer of RBC, Dave Mckay, is a former computer programmer who has a track record of heavy investment into AI. Could he be carving a path towards a more technologically-driven bank?

In fall of 2017, RBC launched NOMI, an AI-driven application developed to examine day-to-day transactions. NOMI can learn about spending habits using neural nets, and make suggestions for saving that are related to your expenses. NOMI has many similarities to Erica; both services help consumers become more fiscally responsible and organized with their finances and daily transactions. RBC’s current AI project is called Borealis, a collection of AI research labs spread across Canada.

Hiring top engineering professors from Canadian Universities, Borealis has the potential to make vast strides in the financial sector. Leonid Sigal, University of British Columbia Professor and Borealis AI researcher, stated: “Borealis AI is a young, vibrant research organization with exceptional technical talent and a unique mandate for balanced curiosity-driven and applied AI research; it’s at the forefront of the Canadian AI ecosystem.” So far, research publications stemming from Borealis have included such topics as machine learning and data visualization.

With labs in cities that include Edmonton, Montreal, Vancouver, and Toronto, RBC is allocating funds towards the research and development of AI. Nadine Renaud-Tinker, President of RBC Quebec, noted that traffic in branches continues to decrease by 6% a year. Lower branch traffic puts pressure on RBC to ensure that they have a strong online platform to satisfy their consumers’ needs.



Finance for the Future


With the emergence of technological developments by banks such as BoA and RBC, it’s becoming clearer that we are in the midst of an industry-wide disruption that will revolutionize the future of finance. Technologies like NOMI and Erica are changing the way consumers interact with the economy and are creating a more seamless experience with financial institutions.
